Common Questions About Pressure Washing

What's the difference between pressure washing and power washing?

Pressure washing uses regular temperature water at high pressure to clean surfaces. Power washing uses heated water, which is more effective for removing grease, oil, and stubborn grime but can damage delicate surfaces. We use the appropriate method for each surface—soft-washing with lower pressure for siding and wood, and higher pressure for concrete and masonry. Our technicians are trained to select the right technique for optimal results without damage.

Will pressure washing damage my siding or deck?

When done correctly by professionals, pressure washing is completely safe for most surfaces. We adjust pressure based on material type—vinyl siding receives gentle soft-washing around 500-1000 PSI, while concrete can handle 3000+ PSI. We keep the wand at proper angles and distances to prevent water intrusion behind siding or wood splintering on decks. Our experience and training ensure safe, effective cleaning without damage.

How often should I have my property pressure washed?

Most homeowners benefit from pressure washing once per year, typically in spring or early summer. However, frequency depends on your environment and property type. Homes with significant tree cover, high humidity, or north-facing surfaces may need twice-yearly cleaning due to mold and algae growth. Commercial properties with high foot traffic often require quarterly service. We can assess your property and recommend an appropriate maintenance schedule.

How much does pressure washing cost in Hamilton?

Pressure washing costs vary by project size and complexity. Driveways typically range $100-$300, house siding $200-$600, decks $150-$400, and patios $100-$350. Factors affecting price include square footage, surface material, accessibility, and level of soiling. We provide free estimates with detailed pricing breakdowns. Many customers save by bundling multiple surfaces or combining pressure washing with window cleaning for package discounts.

What surfaces can be pressure washed safely?

Most exterior surfaces can be safely pressure washed with proper technique: concrete driveways and sidewalks, asphalt, vinyl and aluminum siding, brick, stucco, wood and composite decks, patios, stone pavers, fences, and outdoor furniture. Some delicate surfaces like old wood, painted surfaces with loose paint, or certain types of roofing require gentler soft-washing methods. We evaluate each surface and use appropriate pressure levels and cleaning solutions.
